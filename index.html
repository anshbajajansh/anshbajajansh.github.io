About Millets

Millets are a group of small-seeded grasses that are widely cultivated as cereal crops. They are known for their nutritional value, high protein content, and resilience to harsh weather conditions. Millets are considered as "smart foods" and play a significant role in ensuring food security and nutrition.

Benefits of Millets

  • Rich in dietary fiber
  • Gluten-free and easy to digest
  • High in antioxidants and micronutrients
  • Low glycemic index, suitable for diabetics
  • Environmentally sustainable crop

Celebrate the International Year of Millets by incorporating these nutritious grains into your diet and promoting their cultivation and consumption worldwide.
